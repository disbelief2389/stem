
# stem
>"StemHub"

A git/GitHub parallel for open-source music production, currently in a pure ideation or pre-development phase. 
## Download and Edit the Mindmap
1. **Download & Install Freeplane:** The initial mindmaps were made using Freeplane, which is a free and open-source software. You can download Freeplane from their official site  [here](https://docs.freeplane.org/getting-started/getting-started.html).
2. **Download the Mindmap:** Once you have installed Freeplane, clone the repository, or simply download the .mm file from the latest release.
3. **Open the Mindmap:** Open the mindmap in Freeplane and edit/view.
4. **Submit your own version:** Submit a pull request with your modified changes.
## Mindmap Excerpt
>Refer to the mindmap resources for a more complete understanding.
### What?
- A git/GitHub parallel for music production
- The foundation for an open-source approach to music production 
- A proposal for new methods
- Cloud-based version control
- Inevitable
- Logical
- Mass collaboration
- Music projects without any centralised ownership
- Necessary
- Possible
- Revocation of monetary incentive

### When?
- Now
- The future
- Yesterday
- (literally a timeless idea)

### Who?
- Helps to lower the bar for newer producers
- Honest musicians and artists
- Threatens music contractors' $$$

### How?
- Brainstorming
- Critical and explicit discussions
- Inspire others
- The ripest area for discussion
- Radical self-reliance and defiance
- Work with software developers

### Where?
- Anywhere and everywhere
- Online and in person

### Why?
- Counterbalance to financial incentives
- Extreme bias towards "music rights" or "music ownership."
- Extreme demand
- Far too many producers gatekeep at the drop of a hat
- The industrialisation of music threatens its future
